# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: A Digital Hygiene Checklist

### **📌 General Digital Safety Tips**

* **1. Strengthen Passwords:** Use a unique, complex passphrase for your student portals and personal emails. Avoid using your date of birth, name, or simple sequences like "123456."
* **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on WhatsApp, Instagram, and LinkedIn. This adds a vital layer of security that prevents account hijacking even if your password is leaked.
* **3. Audit App Permissions:** Regularly check your smartphone settings to see which apps have access to your microphone, camera, and contacts. Delete unnecessary "utility" apps that often contain adware or trackers.
* **4. Verify Official Domains:** Always look for **.gov.in** or **.nic.in** when accessing government services like RTO, Aadhaar, or University portals to avoid phishing clones.
* **5. Secure Public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into banking apps or entering sensitive credentials while using free public Wi-Fi at airports or cafes; always prefer your mobile data for private transactions.
* **6. Think Before You Click:** Hover over links in emails or SMS to see the actual destination URL. If the address looks like a string of random characters or a shortened link you don't recognize, do not click it.

---

### **💸 Financial & UPI Safety**

* **7. The 'Receive' Rule:** Remember that **entering your UPI PIN is only for sending money**, never for receiving it. Scanning a QR code to "receive" funds is a common scam used to drain accounts.
* **8. Verify UPI IDs:** Before hitting 'Pay' for college fests, club events, or local vendors, double-check the verified name displayed on the UPI app (GPay/PhonePe/Paytm) to ensure it matches the intended recipient.

---

### **📞 Emergency Reporting Channels**

If you or a peer encounter a digital threat or financial fraud, use these official channels immediately:

* **National Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial **1930** immediately to report financial fraud.
* **Official Reporting Portal:** [www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)
* **Bank Alert:** Contact your bank’s fraud division within the **"Golden Hour"** (the first 2 hours) to freeze suspicious transactions and protect your balance.
