## 🔍 Part A: Cybercrime Case Study
**Topic:** RTO E-Challan Phishing Scam (Gondia/Bhandara Incident)

* **The Incident:** A victim lost ₹5,00,000 after clicking a malicious link disguised as an official traffic fine notification (E-Challan) from the RTO.
* **Mechanism (Modus Operandi):**
    * **Social Engineering:** Exploiting the victim's fear of legal penalties and urgent deadlines.
    * **Smishing:** Sending fraudulent SMS messages with "spoofed" or look-alike URLs.
    * **Credential Harvesting:** Stealing sensitive UPI and Banking data via fake payment portals.
* **Key Learning:** Technical security measures are ineffective if users are not trained to identify and spot "urgent" social engineering tactics.

## 🛡️ Part B: Stay Safe Online Checklist
To combat the threats identified in Part A, I developed a localized safety checklist featuring 8 actionable tips:

1.  **Financial Security:** The "Golden Rule"—A UPI PIN is **only** for sending money, never for receiving.
2.  **Technical Hardening:** Strict enforcement of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and regular App Permission audits.
3.  **Domain Verification:** Identifying official government portals exclusively via `.gov.in` or `.nic.in` extensions.
4.  **Emergency Channels:** Instant reporting of financial fraud via the **1930** helpline and **cybercrime.gov.in**.
