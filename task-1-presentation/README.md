# Project Portfolio: Student Digital Ambassador

## 📂 Module 1: Digital Literacy Awareness
"Digital Literacy: Your Gateway to Success"

### **1.1 Objective**
To establish a high-impact visual resource that defines digital literacy and provides peers at VIT Bhopal with actionable steps for safe and professional online engagement.

---

### **1.2 Key Topics Covered**
* **Core Definition:** The ability to find, evaluate, and communicate information through digital platforms responsibly and effectively.
* **Essential Apps:** GitHub for hosting technical portfolios, LinkedIn for professional networking, and Google Workspace for academic collaboration.
* **Internet Safety:** Practical protocols for Phishing recognition, securing accounts with Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), and maintaining strict UPI PIN safety.
* **Professionalism:** Strategies for maintaining a formal tone and ensuring academic profiles reflect current institutional details.
* **Reporting Channels:** Essential knowledge of the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal and the 1930 Helpline.

---

### **1.3 Deliverable Details**
* **Tool Used:** Canva.
---

### **1.4 Module Reflection**
Name: Sarvagya Anand | Reg No: 25BCE10648 

As a Student Digital Ambassador at VIT Bhopal, I designed this visual resource to simplify the broad concept of digital literacy for my peers. I chose Canva as my primary tool because its professional templates allowed me to strike a balance between aesthetic appeal and technical depth. The resulting infographic focuses on four critical pillars: foundational definitions, student-centric tools, security protocols (with a focus on UPI safety), and professional branding.

The most interesting part of this task was the process of "information chunking"—taking complex topics like ransomware and distilling them into scannable, high-impact tips. My biggest challenge was ensuring the design remained minimalist while still including mandatory Indian safety resources, such as the 1930 helpline. This project has significantly improved my ability to communicate technical safety information to a non-technical audience.
