## 🚀 Professional Standards Applied
To ensure professional credibility and a high response rate, the following standards were applied to all drafts:

* **The "3-Second" Subject Line:** Clear, concise, and includes Name, Reg No., and specific purpose for instant recognition.
* **Formal Salutations:** Using "Dear Professor [Name]" or "Dear Hiring Team" to maintain academic and corporate decorum.
* **The "Call to Action":** Every email ends with a clear, polite next step to reduce communication gaps.
* **Official Signature:** Including Year, Branch (B.Tech CSE), and University (VIT Bhopal) to provide immediate context.

---

## 🛡️ Digital Footprint Strategy
The **Social Media Checklist** included in this folder is designed to protect a student's "searchability" and online reputation. Key strategies include:

1.  **Privacy Hardening:** Auditing 3rd-party app permissions and post visibility to secure personal data.
2.  **Professional Branding:** Aligning the "Bio" across LinkedIn and GitHub to show a consistent technical identity.
3.  **The "Recruiter Test":** Ensuring that no public content would be uncomfortable to explain during a professional interview.

## 💡 Module Reflections

"In Task 4 Part A, I practiced the art of formal correspondence by drafting two distinct professional emails. The first, a request for an extension on a UHV assignment, taught me how to communicate academic challenges—such as health issues—without compromising my professional reputation. I now understand that showcasing my technical growth and club involvements on platforms like LinkedIn can serve as a powerful 'digital trail' for recruiters. This task has reinforced my responsibility to maintain a respectful online presence and to lead my peers in practicing safe and ethical digital behavior."
